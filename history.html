History of Cathay Pacific

Cathay Pacific was founded on 24 September 1946 by Australian Sydney H. de Kantzow and American Roy C. Farrell. The airline started with a single Douglas DC-3 aircraft and has grown to become one of the world's leading airlines.

In the early years, Cathay Pacific focused on regional routes within Asia. Over time, the airline expanded its network to include destinations in Europe, North America, and beyond.

Throughout its history, Cathay Pacific has been known for its commitment to safety, service, and innovation. The airline has received numerous awards and accolades, including being named the world's best airline multiple times by Skytrax.

Today, Cathay Pacific continues to be a major player in the global aviation industry, with a modern fleet and a strong focus on sustainability and customer experience.
